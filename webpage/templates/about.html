Phishing Guidebook

The essential notes to identifying and avoiding Phishing Attacks:

  • Phishing Attacks are also known as Social Engineering Attacks
  • Common types of Phishing Attacks include Spear Phishing, Whaling, Smishing, Vishing and Email Phishing
  • Email Phishing is the most common of all and occurs when an attacker manage to obtain an individual's email address
  • Factors in an attacker gaining ones email address include Data Breaches, Dumpster Diving, Weak Passwords or Cloning an email sent by a trusted organisational body
  • The Attacker that sent the message often writes it to envoke a sense of urgency from the user, for example they might be informing them that their password has expired and they must click on the provided link
  • An effective countermeasure against Phishing Attacks is to hover over any provided links to check the destination
  • If the destination can be reached by search engine, it would be advised over clicking the link
  • Other types of Phishing Attacks include Search Engine Phishing, Clone Phishing, HTTPS Phishing, Pop-Up Phishing and Evil Twin Phishing
  • Be sure to have Firewalls installed on your devices
